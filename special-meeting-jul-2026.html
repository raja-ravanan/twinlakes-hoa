Section 2

Pond Management Update

Background

The Board reviewed the history of pond maintenance following the transition from the developer.

It was explained that previous maintenance contracts did not include the level of service ultimately required for all three ponds. During this year's budgeting process, the Board solicited multiple proposals for comprehensive pond management.

Due to limited operating funds and reserve balances, the Board selected a lower-cost vendor after reviewing pricing and publicly available references. The Board explained that the decision was made in an effort to balance fiscal responsibility with expected service levels.

During the spring season, the Board monitored the vendor's performance and communicated concerns regarding the condition of the ponds. As conditions continued to deteriorate and responsiveness declined, the Board terminated the contract and requested proposals from additional qualified pond management companies.

Following onsite evaluations and proposal reviews, the Board selected Jones Lake Management to provide services for the remainder of the season.

Pond Management Presentation

Jones Lake Management provided an overview of the current condition of the ponds. The representative explained that:

  • The small pond is significantly different from the two larger lakes.
  • The small pond is shallow and receives substantial stormwater runoff.
  • Nutrient loading from runoff, organic material, fertilizer, and other environmental factors contributes to accelerated vegetation growth.
  • The larger lakes currently require only limited maintenance and do not require aggressive treatment.

The representative explained the current treatment strategy, which consists of treating portions of the pond at a time in order to prevent oxygen depletion and reduce the risk of fish kills.

Estimated visible improvement was projected within approximately 6–8 weeks, with continued improvement expected through consistent maintenance.

Resident Discussion

Residents asked numerous questions regarding: causes of the current pond conditions; previous maintenance decisions; construction runoff; pond dye; grass carp; aeration; bubblers; mosquito control; grass clippings and fertilizer practices; dredging; MSD requirements; and long-term maintenance planning.

The vendor explained that while several environmental factors have contributed to current conditions, long-term improvement will require continued maintenance combined with nutrient reduction and other best management practices.

The Board emphasized that no single factor caused the current condition and acknowledged that lessons were learned during the transition between vendors.

Board Direction
  • Jones Lake Management will continue providing biweekly treatments.
  • Progress will continue to be monitored throughout the season.
  • Long-term improvement options, including aeration and other capital improvements, will continue to be evaluated as funding permits.
  • Pond depth measurements will be obtained to assist with future planning.
  • Landscaping practices around the ponds will continue to be reviewed.
Important Note

No formal Board action was requested or taken.

Section 3

Financial Report

The Treasurer presented an independently prepared financial worksheet for discussion.

During the presentation, several homeowners noted that the figures differed from the monthly financial reports regularly prepared by Mulloy Properties.

The Board President clarified that:

  • The worksheet presented during the meeting had not previously been presented to or reviewed by the Board.
  • The Board's monthly financial oversight has been based upon the financial reports prepared by Mulloy Properties, which serve as the Association's official management financial reports.
  • Based upon those reports, the Board had not concluded that the Association had materially exceeded its approved operating budget.

The Treasurer explained that the worksheet was developed using invoices, bank statements, and other financial information that he was independently reconciling and stated that additional reconciliation work remained.

Several homeowners expressed confusion regarding the differences between the two financial presentations and requested clarification regarding which information represented the Association's official financial position.

The Board explained that differences may exist because of reconciliation timing, reporting periods, invoice timing, or differing methodologies. The Board agreed that reconciliation should occur before any conclusions are drawn regarding the Association's financial position.

Financial Oversight Process

The Board reviewed its existing financial oversight procedures, including:

  • Monthly review of management financial reports.
  • Invoice review and approval.
  • Budget monitoring.
  • Multiple approval levels prior to payment.
  • Ongoing reserve monitoring.

The Board also noted that duplicate invoices and billing discrepancies had been identified and corrected during the normal review process.

Budget Status

The Board reported that:

  • HOA operating expenses generally remain within budget.
  • Garden Home operating expenses continue to be monitored.
  • Higher-than-anticipated expenses have primarily resulted from snow removal, insurance, irrigation maintenance, and utility costs.
  • Reserve funding has continued during the year.

The Board reiterated its commitment to managing Association funds prudently while maintaining required community services.

Section 4

HOA Tax ID Structure

The Board explained that Twin Lakes was established as a single legal entity by the developer.

Questions were raised regarding whether Garden Homes and Patriot Homes should operate under separate tax identification numbers.

The Board explained that maintaining a single entity provides:

  • Lower administrative costs.
  • Simplified accounting.
  • Reserve tax advantages.
  • Single insurance structure.
  • Shared responsibility for common areas and amenities.

The Board further explained that creating separate entities would introduce additional legal, financial, accounting, insurance, and governance complexities.

Important Note

No Board action was taken.

Section 5

Election Process

The Board presented a preliminary roadmap for the 2026 Board election process. The anticipated schedule includes:

  • Early Fall — Election notification distributed by Mulloy Properties.
  • September — Candidate nominations.
  • October — In-person election.
  • January 1, 2027 — Beginning of new Board term.

The Board explained that additional election procedures remain under review to ensure compliance with the governing documents.

Residents suggested several ideas for future consideration, including staggered Board terms and improved transition planning. The Board acknowledged these suggestions and indicated they would be reviewed as part of future governance discussions.

Important Note

No changes to election procedures were adopted during this meeting.

Section 6

HOA Website

The Board introduced the newly developed HOA website. The website is intended to improve communication, transparency, and homeowner access to Association information. Current features include:

  • Governing documents.
  • Vendor directory.
  • Board information.
  • Community updates.
  • Online homeowner request submission.
  • Resource library.

The Board encouraged residents to utilize the website as the primary source for Association information, and indicated additional enhancements will continue to be added.

Section 7

Landscaping and Common Area Maintenance

Residents raised concerns regarding: grass cutting; weed control; common area maintenance; tree replacement; shrub maintenance; and street lighting.

The Board agreed to review reported maintenance concerns with the applicable contractors and inspect specific locations identified by homeowners.

The Board also clarified maintenance responsibilities for Garden Homes and Patriot Homes where applicable under the governing documents.

Section 8

Communication

Several homeowners requested more frequent communication from the Board. The Board responded that communication currently includes:

  • Community newsletters.
  • Board meetings.
  • HOA website updates.
  • Correspondence through Mulloy Properties.

The Board stated that the new website is intended to improve transparency by providing centralized access to Association information and documents.

Section 9

Resident Questions

Additional homeowner questions and comments addressed: the Kentucky Planned Community Act; governing documents; committee development; vendor oversight; contract management; tree replacement responsibilities; irrigation; snow removal; street lighting; and community maintenance standards.

The Board responded to questions as time permitted and noted several items for future follow-up.

Summary

Summary of Board Direction

During the meeting, the Board confirmed its intent to:

  • Continue Jones Lake Management's current treatment program.
  • Monitor pond recovery throughout the season.
  • Evaluate future long-term pond improvement projects.
  • Reconcile financial reports presented during the meeting with the Association's official management financial reports.
  • Continue prudent financial oversight.
  • Proceed with election planning for Fall 2026.
  • Continue expanding the HOA website.
  • Review landscaping and maintenance concerns raised by residents.
Important Note

No formal motions or votes requiring Board action were taken during this meeting.

Adjournment

There being no further business, the meeting was adjourned. The Board thanked the residents for their attendance, participation, and questions, and encouraged homeowners to continue using the HOA website and established communication channels to stay informed and submit future requests.
